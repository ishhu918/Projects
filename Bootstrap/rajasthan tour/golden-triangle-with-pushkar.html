Golden Triangle with Pushkar Tour

  1. Home
  2. Golden Triangle with Pushkar Tour

Tour Description:

The Golden Triangle of Northern India offers a splendid view barren desert, the sand dunes and the magnificent monuments that stands tall as the epitome of the rich culture of the bygone era of Rajasthan. In this tour, we will discover the magic of the Golden Triangle of Northern India visiting Pushkar, Ajmer, Jaipur, Delhi and Agra for an unforgettable experience that will last for lifetime. Through our journey, we will come across the charm and elegance of these beautiful cities and explore its age-old tradition and culture.


Pushkar is one of the major pilgrimage sites for Hindu community people across the country and Ajmer serves as a holy city for the Muslim community people. The two cities are unique in their own color and custom. One will get the opportunity to experience the rural side of Rajasthan by visiting Pushkar whereas the Ajmer city provides a serene climate and holistic approach with the surrounding Aravali hills.


Both the cities, Pushkar and Ajmer, have a number of beautiful temples and Ghats. The view of people taking a holy dip at the Ghats at the wee hours in the morning is simple amazing. The festivity and fairs arranged in Pushkar take you to the real India and the people living here. The vibrant mosques of Ajmer with fine architectures symbolize its historical significance. A temple dedicated to Lord Brahma is located in Pushkar. The evening time brings joy and entertainment with the traditional music, dance and other cultural events along with the prayers from the temples and mosques.


We will head to our next destination, the capital city of the state of Rajasthan. Jaipur, also known as the ‘Pink City’, is a perfect combination of traditional architects and modern values. The majestic monuments, enchanting havelis and beautiful forts make it the most visited place in Rajasthan. Traditional artifacts and the glory of the past still hold its importance in the present time.

We head for Agra next. Here we get to see the Taj Mahal, one of the Seven Wonders of the World. The breath taking monument made by Shah Jahan in the fond memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal stands as an epitome of love and romance. After touring Agra, we proceed to the capital city of the country, New Delhi.

Duration

7 Nights / 8 Days

Destination Covered

Delhi ➤ Ajmer ➤ Pushkar ➤ Jaipur ➤ Agra ➤ Delhi

Day 1 ➤ Delhi
After arriving at the Delhi airport, our representative will escort you to the hotel and help you settle with the transfer and check-in process. We will relax the rest of the day at the hotel room before we start the Golden Triangle tour the next morning. We will spend the night at the hotel.
Day 2 ➤ Delhi - Ajmer - Pushkar
We will head to Ajmer the next morning by taking a train journey. After reaching Ajmer, we will first visit the tomb of the Khwaja Moinuddin Chisti. Khwaja Moinuddin Chisti was a famous Muslim saint. We pay a visit to the Adhai Din Ka Jhonpara and Taragarh Fort after touring the Dargah Shareef. We will head for Pushkar next for the night stay. It is only a 20 minutes drive to reach Pushkar where arrangements will be done at the hotel for an overnight stay.
Day 3 ➤ Pushkar - Jaipur
Early morning, we proceed to the capital city of Rajasthan, Jaipur. The Pink City has the only Brahma temple of the country which is quite unique in itself. The city also houses some of the beautiful temples located on the banks of the lake. The city is considered to be one of the hottest tourist places as hundreds of thousands of tourist visit Jaipur every year. We relax at the hotel room for the rest of the day before proceeding for a daylong tour to the city the next morning.
Day 4 ➤ Jaipur
We visit one of the oldest forts of Jaipur, the amber fort. We tour the fort by taking an elephant ride, just the way the royals of Rajasthan do. The fort which is just 11 kms away from the main city is a ‘Must See’ place in Jaipur. The major highlights of Jaipur include Hawa Mahal, Jantar Mantar and City Palace Museum. After visiting these places in the afternoon, we return back to the hotel for an overnight stay.
Day 5 ➤ Jaipur - Fatehpur Sikri - Agra
Next morning, we proceed to Agra, a city that holds one of the seven wonders across the globe. On the way, we take a break at Fatehpursikri where we pay a visit to the Tomb of Salim Chisti and Jama Masjid. We tour the Taj Mahal in Agra, also popularly called the Crowning Jewel of India. We spend the night at the hotel in Agra.
Day 6 ➤ Agra - Delhi
We visit the Taj Mahal the following day. Built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in the memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal, Taj Mahal takes you back to the golden era of Indian culture. Made with white marble stone the monument is an iconic structure and pride of the nation worldwide. The charm and elegance of Taj Mahal make you mesmerized and spellbound. It was also declared as a World Heritage Site by UNESCO.

The Agra Fort is located a few kilometers away from the Taj Mahal. We visit the Agra Fort before leaving for Delhi. After reaching the capital city, we spend the night at the hotel before proceeding for a daylong trip to the city the next morning.

Day 7 ➤ Delhi
Delhi, the capital city of the country, has historical significance form the age-old period. The city has been the central power and headquarters of the Indian democracy. The major sightseeing in Delhi includes Red Fort, Qutub Minar, India Gate, Jama Masjid, Chandni Chowk, Birla Mandir, Humayun’s Tomb and Raj Ghat. We visit these historical monuments and settle ourselves in the evening for a light and sound show at the Red Fort. We return back to the hotel at night.
Day 8 ➤ Delhi
A memorable India representative will drop you at the Delhi airport or railway station for your way back home. We hope, you will find the whole trip exciting and memorable.
